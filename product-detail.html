Why is there not one wavelength?

A new addition to the medical products manufactured by

Lasotronix is ​​the two-wavelength, universal SMART M 980/1470nm surgical laser, which, in addition to phlebology and proctology, is widely used in dermatosurgery, otolaryngology and gynecology.

Additionally, SMART diode lasers can be extended with an additional wavelength of 635nm, which, thanks to biomodulation effects, has a very beneficial effect on blood morphology. Treatments using this wavelength significantly improve soft tissue regeneration, rebuild blood vessels, reduce postoperative pain and swelling, stimulate blood and lymph flow, and strengthen local immunity. The 635nm laser is particularly recommended for patients with inflammation, difficult-to-heal wounds, or ulcers, and after any procedure to accelerate healing and reduce pain and swelling.

For minimally invasive intravenous laser ablation – EVLT, Lasotronix offers diode lasers emitting radiation at various wavelengths: 980nm, 1470nm or 1940nm with the possibility of further expansion with additional light sources.

As the graph shows, for 980nm radiation, the absorption coefficient is higher for hemoglobin than for water. At 1470nm, and especially at 1940nm, the opposite is true, with water absorbing light radiation many times better than hemoglobin. Both of these chromatophores are components of human tissue, including blood and vessel walls. 980nm lasers have been widely used for over 20 years, with an average power of 10-12W during procedures. At 1470nm, lower power levels are required—6-8W, while at 1940nm, 4-6W is sufficient to perform effective surgery. Reducing the amount of power required makes these lasers much safer and more predictable tools for physicians, while also providing patients with a faster and more comfortable recovery.

  • Laser type: diode
  • Output power: up to 15 W for wavelengths 980nm, 1470nm

  • Output power: 0.5 W for wavelengths 405nm, 635nm,
