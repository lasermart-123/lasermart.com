LASER TREATMENT FOR PROCTOLOGY

Haemorrhoids (Laser Hemorrhoidopexy)

Under local or general anaesthesia, laser energy is delivered directly into the haemorrhoidal tissue using a specially designed Bare tip Fiber. The controlled laser energy coagulates the blood vessels supplying the haemorrhoid, causing the tissue to shrink from within while preserving the surrounding mucosa and anal sphincter.

Unlike conventional excisional surgery, laser treatment minimizes tissue damage and promotes natural fibrotic reconstruction, helping secure the mucosa to the underlying tissue. This reduces the likelihood of prolapse and recurrence while preserving normal anatomy.

The procedure is typically completed within a few minutes and offers several clinical advantages, including:

  • Minimally invasive treatment
  • Reduced intraoperative bleeding
  • Minimal postoperative pain
  • Preservation of the anal sphincter and surrounding tissues
  • Faster recovery and earlier return to daily activities
  • Lower risk of postoperative complications
  • Excellent cosmetic and functional outcomes

Fistula

Under the LA/GA, Laser energy is delivered by radial fiber into the anal fistula tract and is used to thermally ablate and close of the abnormal pathway. The laser energy induces destruction of the fistula epithelium and simultaneous obliteration of the remaining fistula tract by a shrinkage effect. The epithelialized tissue is being destroyed in a controlled

way and the fistula tract collapses to a very high degree. This also supports and accelerates the healing process. The advantage of using diode laser using radial fiber compared to conventional surgery is that it gives good control to operator and allows use in convoluted tract. Surgical procedure can be executed in few minutes.

Fissure

The procedure is done under LA-local anaesthesia, it does not require hospitalization of a patient. By the use of laser the pathological tissue of anal fissure is vaporized. The laser removal of a fissure has great efficiency rates. It is the most modern technique used to treat a fissure. The laser surgery is the perfect alternate for other methods.

Pilonidal Sinus

Contrary to conventional methods, laser treats pilonidal sinuses in depth. In the treatment of complete healing and restoration of pilonidal cyst there is no serious risk of recurrence. Recurrences are rare.
